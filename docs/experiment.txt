Experiment 1 :

Consider N players and M arms

The M arms are characterised by a mean m_i and sd sd_i 


The exploration phase : 

For each player -- they cycle through the N arms IN order but not all starting at 1. So player 1 starts at maybe 1 and goes till M, player 2 may start at 6 and go to M, then back 1-5 and so on

This phase is done say k times

Rewards : Equal splits : If p players land on arm a - their reward is sampled from Gamma(m_a, sd_a) OR Unif(m_a, sd_a) as chosen initially and if that reward is r, each of the p players gets an equal r / p

Each player MUST maintain, for themselves, the estimated mean and sd of each arm. Note that OFCOURSE this can be slightly different


The strategy phase : Depending on whether we choose to be eps-greedy or UCB1, in this phase -- each player now uses that strategy to decide what arm to pull and reaps the corresponding reward.

As usual, when collisions happenm rewards are split ; if no collisions, full reward.

We track : at every time step from the sxtart -- total reward of each player ; how many times each arm has been played till a time t AND how many times there have been collisions on an arm till time t; 


ORACLE ::

N < K and oracle
When num players < num arms, oracle maximises total reward by occupying the best K arms by expected mean and sd which it knows. 
N>K, All arms are occupied simply because of the equal split arrangement of rewards - the best one can do there for cumulative reward is the sum of all expected rewards (all rewards are positive)

Mean oracle reward at a time t = oracel reward at t / N
There is therefore the notion of a player regret relative to this reward if we want to plot and see that over time, there is also the more robust mean player reward at t against the mean oracle reward at t for a mean regret over time
